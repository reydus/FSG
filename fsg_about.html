AN INTRODUCTION TO FSG

We are a group of students with a keen interest in both flying and simulation. We have 3 fixed based simulators: A British Aerospace Jetstream 41, a FastJet and a Piper PA-38 Tomahawk in our room, which are maintained by us. The simulators are available 9am to 5pm for any student, even if you aren't studying engineering.

We have meetings every Wednesday where we have flying exercises and continue with the engineering tasks for the simulators, as well as meetings every month to discuss ideas and exercises for the following month. We're a very community oriented society so we regularly have pizza nights and socials.

We are located in the Pilot Studies lab, Room G05.

THE HISTORY OF FSG

FSG was formed in 2010 under the name of SSG, the Student Simulation Group, with the goal of building the Piper PA-38 Tomahawk simulator. In October 2012, the FastJet shell was acquired and work begun to turn it into a fully functioning simulator.

In late 2014 we acquired Room 183 which became our operating base and the JetSim was moved in, with FSG's supervisor Roy Coates having spent over 140 hours building the frame and instrument panels. The next 2 years involved improving the JetSim along with the other 2 sims, and in late 2016 we moved into our new room, G05. From here we're looking to improve the FastJet and the Tomahawk, with both of them getting new instruments and displays.
